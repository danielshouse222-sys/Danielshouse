Privacy Policy.

Plain English. No dark patterns. Your data, your call.

Last Updated · May 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Daniel's House ("we," "us," "our") collects, uses, and protects information when you visit danielshousewellness.com or buy from us. By using the site, you agree to the practices described here. If you don't agree, please don't use the site.

The short version: we collect the information needed to ship your order, run our business, and reach out about products you've asked to hear about. We never sell your data. You can request a copy or deletion any time by emailing privacy@danielshousewellness.com.

What we collect

Information you provide

When you create an account, place an order, sign up for our newsletter, or contact us, you give us:

  • Name, email, shipping and billing address, phone number (optional)
  • Payment information — processed and stored by our payment processor, not by us
  • Order history and product preferences
  • Any information you submit in messages, reviews, or quiz responses

Information collected automatically

When you browse the site, we and our analytics partners collect:

  • Device, browser, and operating system information
  • IP address and approximate location (city/country level)
  • Pages visited, links clicked, and time spent on pages
  • Referring website or marketing source
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies (see below)

How we use it

  • Fulfill your order — process payments, ship products, send shipping and delivery updates, handle returns
  • Account and support — manage your account, answer questions, troubleshoot issues
  • Subscriptions — process recurring shipments, send renewal reminders, let you skip or cancel
  • Marketing (only with consent) — send our newsletter, new product announcements, and occasional offers. You can unsubscribe from every email or by emailing us
  • Improve the site — understand what's working, fix what isn't, build features people actually use
  • Prevent fraud — detect suspicious activity, verify identity for unusual orders
  • Legal compliance — meet tax, accounting, and consumer-protection obligations

Who we share with

We don't sell your personal information. We share it only with service providers we need to run the business, and only the minimum required:

  • Payment processors (Stripe, Shopify Payments, or similar) — to charge your card securely
  • Shipping carriers (UPS, USPS, Canada Post, FedEx) — to deliver your order
  • Email service providers — to send transactional and (with consent) marketing email
  • Analytics providers (such as Google Analytics) — to understand site usage in aggregate
  • Customer support tools — to respond to your questions and store conversation history
  • Legal authorities — only when legally required (subpoena, court order, fraud investigation)

Every service provider is contractually required to protect your information and use it only for the purpose we've engaged them.

Cookies and tracking

We use cookies and similar technologies to keep you logged in, remember your cart, measure site performance, and run marketing campaigns. Most browsers let you block or delete cookies in settings — note that blocking essential cookies may break parts of the site (cart, checkout, account).

We honor the Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal as a request to opt out of "sale" or "sharing" of personal information under California law.

Your rights

Regardless of where you live, you can request to:

  • Access — receive a copy of the personal information we hold about you
  • Correct — fix anything inaccurate
  • Delete — have us erase your information (with limited exceptions for legal/financial records we're required to keep)
  • Opt out of marketing — unsubscribe at any time from any email or by request
  • Port your data — receive your data in a portable format

If you're in California (CCPA / CPRA)

California residents have specific rights including the right to know what categories of personal information we collect, to whom we disclose it, and the right to opt out of any sale or sharing. We don't sell personal information. To exercise any right, email privacy@danielshousewellness.com with "CA Privacy Request" in the subject.

If you're in the EU / UK (GDPR)

You have the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict, port, and object to processing of your personal data. The legal basis for our processing is: contract (to fulfill orders), legitimate interest (to run our business and prevent fraud), and consent (for marketing email). You may lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority.

Data security

We use TLS encryption for all data in transit, strong access controls, and reputable service providers (Stripe, Shopify, etc.) for sensitive operations. No method of transmission is 100% secure, but we take reasonable steps to protect your information. If we ever experience a data breach involving your personal information, we'll notify you and applicable regulators as required by law.

Data retention

We keep order and account information for as long as you have an account with us, and for a period afterward to meet legal, tax, and accounting requirements (typically 7 years). Marketing email lists are kept until you unsubscribe. You can request deletion of your account and associated information at any time.

Children's privacy

This site is not intended for children under 16. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 16. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we'll delete it.

International transfers

Daniel's House operates from North America. If you access the site from outside North America, your information will be transferred to and processed in the United States and Canada. We use Standard Contractual Clauses and equivalent safeguards where required.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as our practices or applicable law changes. The "Last Updated" date at the top reflects the most recent revision. Material changes will be highlighted on the site for 30 days. Continuing to use the site after changes means you accept the updated policy.

Contact us
