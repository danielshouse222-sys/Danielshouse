Pricing & Value

Why are Daniel's House products priced higher than drugstore skincare?+
Because the formulas are different. Drugstore products are built around a target retail price — the formula gets engineered backward from that price, which usually means under-dosing the actives, substituting cheaper ingredient forms (magnesium oxide instead of glycinate, ascorbyl palmitate instead of L-ascorbic acid), and padding the bottle with fillers. Daniel's House is built the other way around: we pick clinically-validated active forms at the doses used in the original studies, manufacture at FDA-registered facilities that produce formulas for leading DTC brands stocked at Sephora, and the price reflects what that costs. A $14 drugstore vitamin C serum and our $34 Boost aren't the same product at different prices — they're different products. The price gap is the active-ingredient cost gap. The same logic applies on the supplement side: cheaper ashwagandha is generic powder, cheaper magnesium is the oxide form that mostly passes through unabsorbed, cheaper probiotics die in stomach acid. We pay for the forms that actually work.
How does Daniel's House compare to brands like SkinCeuticals, Drunk Elephant, Sunday Riley, or Glossier?+
We sit in the same formulation tier and underprice the brand-name luxury comparison. The clearest example: Defense uses the C E Ferulic architecture — L-ascorbic acid + ferulic acid + vitamin E at the concentrations validated in the foundational 2005 Duke University study. SkinCeuticals owns that name and charges $185 for it. Drunk Elephant's equivalent (C-Firma) is $80. Defense is $74 with niacinamide and panthenol added on top. Bounce stacks five actives (retinol + Vit C + HA + Matrixyl peptides + botanical complex) where Drunk Elephant's A-Passioni is a single-active 1% retinol at $74. Sunday Riley's flagship lactic acid treatment (Good Genes) is $85; our Renewal multi-action peeling pads are $48. Glossier's positioning is different — they sell a lifestyle aesthetic at a more accessible price point, but their formulations are simpler and the marketing-spend-to-ingredient-spend ratio is higher than ours. Where we save money is not on the formulas — it's on the brand-marketing markup that adds 50-100% to luxury skincare prices and the lifestyle-positioning markup that adds 30-50% to category-defining DTC brands.
How is the most expensive product (Defense at $74) different from a $30 vitamin C serum?+
Three things. (1) Active form: Defense uses 15% pure L-ascorbic acid — the gold-standard vitamin C form that's been clinically validated since the 1990s. Most $30 serums use cheaper derivatives (ascorbyl palmitate, sodium ascorbyl phosphate) which are more stable in formula but don't deliver the same photoprotection. (2) Stabilization architecture: Defense includes 0.5% ferulic acid, which doubles the photoprotection of vitamin C and vitamin E according to the foundational research. Most affordable vitamin C serums skip the ferulic acid because it's expensive. (3) Supporting actives: Defense adds 4% niacinamide, 1% vitamin E, and 1% panthenol — three additional clinically-validated actives at clinical doses. A $30 vitamin C serum is one active at an uncertain dose; Defense is four actives at validated doses. The price difference is the ingredient cost difference.

How long should one bottle last me?+
Most products are sized for a 2-3 month supply at standard daily use. Cleanser (Wash) lasts 2-3 months at twice-daily use. Serums (Boost, Defense, Bounce, Firm, Plump) last 8-12 weeks at pea-size doses applied to face and neck. Moisturizers (Soft, Mist) last 2-3 months. Awake lasts 3-4 months — you use very little. Mask and Polish are weekly-use, so a single bottle lasts 4-6 months. Per-month effective cost across a full routine is therefore lower than the list price suggests — at base subscription pricing, a complete AM routine costs about $74-87 per month.

What is Smart Refill?+
Smart Refill is the smarter way to subscribe to a mixed routine. A standard monthly subscription ships every product every month — but real products don't all run out at the same pace. Monthly sends every product on the same 30-day cycle — simple, predictable, but you over-receive products that last longer than a month. Smart Refill ships each product on its actual usage cycle (30 / 60 / 90 days depending on bottle size) so you only pay for what you'll actually use that month. Lower average monthly bill, less clutter on your shelf, same total products over the year. Choose whichever fits your style — both are available on every routine and most bundles, both can be paused or cancelled anytime from your account.

Ingredients & Safety

Where are products manufactured?+
All Daniel's House products are manufactured in FDA-registered, GMP-certified facilities in the USA. Every batch is third-party tested for purity, potency, and contamination before it leaves the warehouse.
Are products vegan?+
Most are vegan — our skincare line is 100% vegan and cruelty-free. In supplements, exceptions include Flow (fish-based omega-3) and Collagen (bovine collagen). Each product page lists this clearly.
Can I use products if I'm pregnant or nursing?+
For skincare, avoid retinol (Bounce) and the resurfacing treatments — Renewal (multi-acid peeling pads) and Clear (2% salicylic). Mask contains salicylic acid at gentle weekly-treatment levels — most providers consider it acceptable but some advise against any salicylic during pregnancy. Fully pregnancy-safe alternatives include Wash, Balance (glycolic at 3.5% — confirm with provider; some recommend avoiding all AHAs), Boost (gentle peptides + hyaluronic), Plump, Defense (Vit C/E/Niacinamide/Ferulic — confirm with provider), Soft (multi-weight HA moisturizer — no retinoids), Mist (pure HA mist — the safest choice), Awake (peptide + DCX complex — no retinol or vitamin-A derivatives), Glow (HA spheres in squalane), Polish (physical exfoliant), and Shield (mineral SPF — pregnancy-essential). For supplements, consult your healthcare provider before starting any new supplement during pregnancy or nursing.
Are products gluten-free?+
Yes — all Daniel's House products are gluten-free. Common allergen warnings (soy, dairy, tree nuts, fish) are listed on each product page where applicable.
Can I take supplements with prescription medication?+
For specific medications, consult your doctor or pharmacist. Supplements that warrant extra caution: Tranquil (ashwagandha) with thyroid medication, Flow (omega-3) with blood thinners, Restore (turmeric) with certain anti-inflammatory drugs. Most stacks are well-tolerated, but your healthcare provider knows your situation best.
How do I store products?+
Skincare: cool, dry place out of direct sunlight. Most products last 6-12 months after opening. Supplements: cool, dry place. No refrigeration required even for our probiotic (Biome uses enteric delivery technology for shelf stability).

Using the Products

In what order should I apply skincare?+
Three rules cover almost everything: thinnest to thickest, water before oil, actives at night, antioxidants in the morning.

AM: Wash → Balance (glycolic exfoliant) → Boost (peptide growth factor) → Plump (on damp skin) → Defense (CE Ferulic) → Soft (moisturizer) → Shield (mineral SPF).

PM: Wash → Renewal (multi-acid peeling pads) or Bounce (5-active retinol) — pick one, alternate nights → Firm (peptides) → Mist (HA mist) → Awake (peptide serum) → Glow (HA-in-squalane seal). Add Mask or Polish 1–2x/week, replacing your treatment that night.

Never stack retinol and AHA the same night — your barrier will tell you why.
How long until I see results?+
It depends on the product. Sleep and energy supplements often shift within 1-2 weeks. Skincare changes are typically visible at 4-6 weeks for tone/texture, 8-12 weeks for fine line reduction. Longevity supplements compound over months and years. Consistency beats intensity — daily use is the variable that matters most.
Can I combine multiple products?+
Yes — most are designed to be combined. Our routines and bundles are pre-built combinations Daniel actually uses. The main rules: don't double up on retinol products in skincare (Bounce + Renewal alternate, never together); follow caffeine cutoff times for stim-containing supplements like Surge.
Do I need to take supplements with food?+
Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K) absorb best with meals containing some fat. This includes Multi, Sunshine, Flow, and Vitality. Calm and Synapse can be taken with or without food. Each product page specifies the optimal timing.
What if a product doesn't work for me?+
Send it back within 30 days for a full refund. Wellness is individual — what works for thousands may not be right for you. We'd rather you find a product that does work than feel stuck with one that doesn't.
