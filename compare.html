How we compare.

An honest look at how Daniel's House stacks up against the brands we get compared to most.

Wellness is a crowded shelf. We get asked all the time how we're different from AG1, Ritual, Hims, or The Ordinary — fair question. Here's the short answer: we're a different shape, not necessarily a better brand than any of them at the thing they do best. We do both supplements and skincare, in routines that compound, with clinical-dose forms and full transparency. If that's not what you want, one of the brands below probably is — and we'll happily point you to them.

The Long Version

Where each brand wins.

An honest take on what these brands do well, and where Daniel's House does it differently.

Premium All-In-One Greens

Daniel's House vs. AG1 (Athletic Greens)

Where AG1 Wins

Simplicity. One scoop, one product, one decision. If you want a single-step morning supplement and you don't want to think about it again, AG1 is genuinely good at being that.

Brand and ubiquity. Heavily marketed, athlete-endorsed, broadly available. The decision feels safe even when the data behind it is mixed.

Travel-friendly. Single sachet packs are the most travel-friendly format in the category.

Where We Differ

Clinical doses, not blend doses. AG1's "proprietary blend" contains 70+ ingredients in a small per-serving footprint. We separate ingredients into single-purpose products so each one is at its study dose — magnesium glycinate at 200mg, KSM-66 ashwagandha at 600mg, NAD+ at 250mg. You get a few products, not a blend, but you get the doses the research actually validated.

Inside and outside. AG1 is supplements only. Our daily routines include the skincare layer, because we think most "wellness" stops at the neck and shouldn't.

No subscription lock. Buy one bottle once, or subscribe and save 10%. No subscription minimum, no required commitment.

Transparent Multivitamins

Daniel's House vs. Ritual

Where Ritual Wins

Transparency was the starting point. Ritual was an early voice for label honesty — naming ingredient sources, formulation reasoning, third-party testing. They pushed the category forward.

Beautiful product design. The capsule, the bottle, the packaging — everything is unmistakably theirs. Few brands feel this considered.

Gendered formulation. Their women's-specific and pregnancy lines are some of the best-formulated in the multivitamin category.

Where We Differ

A full system, not a multivitamin. Ritual's catalog is anchored on the multi. Ours is built around routines — a multi plus the supporting actives that the multi alone doesn't deliver at clinical levels (NAD+, ashwagandha, omega-3, magnesium glycinate, creatine).

Skincare in the same house. Ritual doesn't do skincare. Our routines treat the surface and the system as one protocol.

Bundle architecture. Ritual sells individual SKUs. We pre-bundle by goal (Daily, Workout, Longevity, Glow) and by concern (Anti-Aging, Sleep, Energy, Acne) — and let you build your own at a tiered discount.

Wellness Telehealth Platforms

Daniel's House vs. Hims & Hers

Where Hims Wins

Prescription access. Hims and Hers can prescribe — minoxidil for hair loss, sildenafil for ED, GLP-1s for weight, sertraline for mental health. That's a category we don't and won't operate in.

Telehealth integration. Talking to a provider, getting a prescription, and receiving the product all happen inside one platform. For Rx needs, that's hard to beat.

Scale and distribution. Operating coast-to-coast with state-level pharmacy compliance is a real operational moat.

Where We Differ

OTC, premium, transparent. Everything we sell is over-the-counter and consumer-facing. No subscription gating around a doctor visit, no Rx, no consultations required. You see exactly what's in the bottle and decide for yourself.

Considered formulation, not category-blanketing. Hims-Hers' OTC wellness range is broad but generic — vitamin gummies, hair gummies, ashwagandha gummies, melatonin gummies. Our supplements are studied forms at clinical doses, no gummy fillers.

Founder voice. Daniel takes everything we sell. Hims is a publicly traded company with a different brand-customer relationship. Different sizes, different feels.

Skincare-Only Specialists

Daniel's House vs. The Ordinary & Necessaire

Where They Win

The Ordinary on price. Single-actives at honest prices — niacinamide 10% for under $10, retinol for $15. Unbeatable on dollar-per-active in skincare. If budget is the top constraint, they're the answer.

Necessaire on design and body care. Beautifully packaged, body-first, gender-neutral. Their bath oil and body serum are genuinely category-leading.

Skincare specialization. Both brands are deep in skincare and don't dilute focus by selling supplements or wellness adjacents.

Where We Differ

Routine over actives. The Ordinary asks you to assemble your own routine from 60+ products and figure out interactions. We pre-build the AM and PM routine and explain exactly how each step layers — no decision fatigue, no overlap risks.

Skincare + supplements together. The gut-skin axis, the collagen-from-the-inside half, the magnesium-for-sleep-improves-skin-repair loop — all of it is in scope for us, none of it for skincare-only brands.

Premium without the luxury markup. Bundle pricing puts our per-product cost into a range that's premium-grade but not luxury-priced. Necessaire is closer to luxury; we sit between.
