The House · Accessibility

Built for everyone.

Our commitment to making the site usable for all.

Last Updated · May 2026

Daniel's House is committed to making our website accessible to as many people as possible — including people with disabilities and people using assistive technology. We treat accessibility as part of the product, not an afterthought.

Our standard

We aim to meet Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA — the international standard for web accessibility, also referenced by the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and similar laws in Canada, the UK, and the EU. We test our pages against this standard and treat any gap as a bug to fix.

What we've built in

  • Semantic HTML — pages use proper headings, landmarks, lists, and labels so screen readers can navigate efficiently
  • Keyboard navigation — every interactive element (nav, buttons, forms, cart, lightboxes, accordions) is reachable and operable with Tab, Enter, and Escape
  • Visible focus states — keyboard focus is always visible
  • Color contrast — body text and interactive elements meet or exceed the WCAG AA contrast ratio (4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components)
  • Alt text on images — product images and meaningful illustrations include descriptive alt text; decorative images are marked as such so screen readers skip them
  • Resizable text — the site supports up to 200% zoom without loss of content or function
  • Form labels and errors — every form field has a visible, programmatically-associated label; validation errors are announced to assistive technology
  • Respect for motion preferences — animations and transitions are minimal and respect the user's prefers-reduced-motion setting
  • Mobile-first design — touch targets are sized for finger use; tap areas don't sit too close together

Known limitations

We're a young company and we know accessibility is never "done." Areas we're actively improving:

  • Some product photos may have shorter alt text than they should — we're auditing and expanding
  • The product carousel/lightbox is being refined for better screen reader announcements
  • Captions for any future video content

If you find something that doesn't work for you, we want to hear about it.

Report an issue

If any part of the site is difficult to use with assistive technology, or you're having trouble accessing information, please contact us. Include:

  • The page URL where you experienced the issue
  • A description of what happened (and what you expected)
  • The assistive technology or browser/device you're using, if you're comfortable sharing
Email: accessibility@danielshousewellness.com
We aim to respond within two business days and to deliver a fix or workaround as quickly as possible. If a fix will take longer, we'll keep you posted on progress.

Alternative access

If you'd prefer to place an order or get product information by email instead of the website, write us at daniel@danielshousewellness.com. We'll walk you through anything you'd like and process the order on your behalf.

Ongoing work

Accessibility audits and improvements are part of every site update. We'll revise this page as we ship improvements or learn from feedback. Thank you for helping us make Daniel's House better.
